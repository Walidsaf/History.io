History of Prophets

Prophet Adam

Prophet Adam is considered the first human being and the first prophet in Islam. According to Islamic tradition, he was created by Allah from clay and was the first to live on earth. He and his wife, Hawwa (Eve), lived in Paradise until they were expelled for disobeying Allah's command.

Prophet Noah

Prophet Noah is known for building a great ark to save his followers and animals from a massive flood sent by Allah. He preached monotheism and warned his people of the impending flood, but only a few believed him.

Prophet Abraham

Prophet Abraham, known as the father of many nations, was a key figure in Islam, Judaism, and Christianity. He is known for his unwavering faith in Allah, and he was willing to sacrifice his son in obedience to Allah's command, a test of faith that Allah ultimately did not require him to complete.

Prophet Moses

Prophet Moses is one of the most important prophets in Islam. He was chosen by Allah to lead the Israelites out of Egypt and received the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai. His life is a series of miracles and trials, demonstrating Allah's power and guidance.

Prophet Jesus

Prophet Jesus, known in Islam as Isa, is considered a significant prophet and messenger of Allah. He performed many miracles by Allah's will and preached about monotheism and morality. Muslims believe he was not crucified but was raised up by Allah and will return before the Day of Judgment.

Early Life of Prophet Muhammad

Date: Approximately 570 CE

Place: Mecca, Saudi Arabia

Born to the Quraysh tribe, Prophet Muhammad's father Abdullah died before his birth. His mother Amina passed away when he was six. He was then raised by his grandfather Abdul Muttalib, and later by his uncle Abu Talib. Muhammad grew up known for his honesty and integrity.

Prophethood

Date: First Revelation around 610 CE

While meditating in the Cave of Hira, Muhammad received his first revelation from the angel Gabriel. This marked the beginning of his prophethood, during which he preached monotheism and moral conduct.

Persecution and Migration

Date: Hijra to Medina in 622 CE

Due to severe persecution in Mecca, Muhammad and his followers migrated to Yathrib (Medina). This migration, known as the Hijra, marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar and the establishment of a Muslim community in Medina.

Medina Period

In Medina, Muhammad established a constitution and formed alliances. Key battles included Badr, Uhud, and the Battle of the Trench. The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah in 628 CE was a significant peace agreement with the Quraysh tribe.

Return to Mecca

Date: 630 CE

Prophet Muhammad and his followers peacefully entered Mecca. The Kaaba was cleansed of idols and rededicated to the worship of Allah. This event consolidated Islam in the Arabian Peninsula.

Death and Legacy

Date: 632 CE

Place: Medina

Prophet Muhammad passed away in Medina and was buried in the chamber of his wife Aisha. He is considered the final prophet in Islam, and his teachings are foundational to the Islamic faith.
